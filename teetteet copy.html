Welcome to the Teet Teet Nation

Born beneath the fireworks of Liberty Municipal Park

The Story

From the back door of a small apartment overlooking Liberty Municipal Park, two generations of Carolina wrens— affectionately known as the Teet Teets—declared their independence. Each July 4th, as fireworks shimmer above the trees, they perform their sacred Peanut Patriotic Ascension, hopping proudly before their human allies and claiming the backyard as sovereign territory.

The Symbol

The flag above represents their unity: two wrens facing the rising sun, peanuts at the center of their alliance, and a phoenix soaring overhead to mark their connection to the greater Phoenix Network. The red, white, and blue ribbons honor the chaos and humor of the American spirit—especially when Michael Berry talks over “Glory Glory Hallelujah.”

The Tradition

Each year, the Teet Teets reconvene at dusk, chirping their anthem as Liberty’s fireworks ignite. Their song reminds us that even the smallest wings can carry the weight of celebration, comedy, and freedom.
